The problem

Saving money as a group is still surprisingly painful.

Group gifts, trip funds, community pots. Someone always ends up chasing transfers across different banks, currencies, and apps, with no guarantee the money stays put until the goal is actually reached.

Fragmented transfers

Different banks, currencies, and apps for every member. Someone becomes the unpaid treasurer.

No real lock

Money sits in one person's account. Nothing stops it from being spent before the goal is hit.

Web3 makes it worse

Crypto tools fix the trust problem but demand a wallet, a chain, and a seed phrase from every member.

The solution

Tugether pools any asset, locks it with the group's own rules, and never shows a single crypto term.

Pool from anywhere

Particle Universal Accounts (EIP 7702) merge deposits from any asset on any chain into one shared balance.

Trustless, group governed lock

ZeroDev smart account permissions hold the funds. Release or refund happens only when the members agree, enforced by the contract itself.

Zero wallet onboarding

Magic turns a Google or email login into a smart account behind the scenes. No seed phrase, ever.

How it is built

One app, real on chain trust, zero backend custody.

Magic

Google or email login becomes the signer. No seed phrase.

Particle Universal Accounts

EIP 7702 upgrade merges any chain deposit into one balance.

ZeroDev

Session keys and permissions deploy and govern each GoalVault.

Arbitrum

Settlement chain for every vault and every deposit.

▲

Next.js + TypeScript

Frontend and API routes in one app, deployed on Vercel.

Supabase

Light metadata only: names, links, cached balances. Never funds.

GoalVault (per goal smart account)

Every member's own smart account signs their deposit. Release or refund only happens once the group votes, on chain, enforced by the contract itself. No admin key, no backend switch.

Why this needs a chain

A spreadsheet split cannot do this.

  • No single person ever holds the group's money
  • Funds cannot move unless the group agrees, by rule, not by promise
  • Any asset, any chain, merges into one balance automatically
  • Every deposit and release is verifiable on Arbitrum
